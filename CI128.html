Dear friends & family,

I humbly ask you to vote no on CI-128, a ballot initiative that enshrines abortion without limits in the Montana constitution. I apologize for adding to this unbearable political season that we're in, but this issue transcends politics and I'm compelled to speak out.

Abortion is already legal in Montana up until the point of 'fetal viability'. By employing vague language, CI-128 enshrines abortion at any stage of pregnancy so long as a "healthcare professional" determines it's necessary to protect the mother's "life or health". Rather than just allowing late-term abortion in the case when a medical doctor deems it necessary to save the mother's life, the language paves the way for any healthcare professional, such as a worker at an abortion clinic, to legally justify an abortion so long as it's ostensibly related to the mother's health (be it physical, mental or otherwise). In essence, it removes any real barrier to late-term abortion and it is a myth that most or all late-term abortions are performed for valid medical reasons across the country. CI-128 should therefore be rejected by anyone except those with the most unbridled pro-choice views.

The marketing campaign in support of CI-128 is impressive and does an excellent job of appealing to our uniquely independent Montana mindset by saying it's all about limiting government overreach and protecting "our" rights. Left unspoken is all of the rights taken away from the unborn, chiefly the right to life itself. Pro-lifers aren't interested in meddling in people's lives, we just want to combat what we see as the greatest social injustice of our time.

If you're still planning to vote for CI-128, please make sure you're not doing it just because you don't identify with (or have grievances against) the traditional pro-life crowd of conservative Christians, Republicans and others on the political right. One good thing about CI-128 is that it's an opportunity for us to vote our conscience on a single issue rather than on a candidate or party. Voting 'no' doesn't mean you're a Republican! It actually doesn't even mean you're pro-life. It just means you think this ballot measure goes too far, or you're not ready to endorse it being added to our state constitution.

Sincerely & respectfully,

Kyle McIntyre
